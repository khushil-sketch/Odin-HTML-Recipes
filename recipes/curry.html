How to make Rajma Chawal

(Kidney Beans Curry)

Why have I included this recipe?

Because Rajma is the GOAT curry, it reminds you of simpler days

Ingredients (how does Copilot know the ingredients for Rajma???)

  • 1 cup rajma (kidney beans)
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 medium tomato, finely chopped
  • 1 green chili, slit
  • 1 inch ginger, finely chopped
  • 4-5 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 black cardamom
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon dry mango powder (amchur)
  • 1/2 teaspoon kasuri methi
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 tablespoon chopped cilantro
  • 1 teaspoon salt

It takes months to gather these ingredients, but trust me, it's all worth it

Instructions - its time to COOK!!

  1. Soak the rajma in enough water overnight or for 8 hours. Drain the water and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pot on medium heat. Once hot, add cumin seeds, bay leaf and black cardamom. Saute for few seconds until cumin seeds splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions, ginger, garlic and green chili. Cook for 2 to 3 minutes until onions soften.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook for 2 minutes until tomatoes are slightly soft.
  5. Then add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder and garam masala. Mix and cook for a minute.
  6. Add the soaked rajma along with 3 cups of water and salt. Stir to combine.
  7. Close the pot with a lid and cook on medium heat for 1 whistle. Then lower the heat to low and cook for 20 minutes.
  8. After 20 minutes, turn off the heat and let the pressure release naturally from the pot.
  9. Open the lid and add dry mango powder, kasuri methi and chopped cilantro. Mix to combine.
  10. Enjoy with rice or naan!
